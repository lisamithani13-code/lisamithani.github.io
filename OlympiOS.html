The primary issue wasn't the venue's setup but a lack of shared atmosphere and social cues. The emotional and social infrastructure was missing, leaving viewers needing contextual understanding, emotional connection, and social synchronization to feel truly engaged.

Developing Initial Concepts

Concept A: The Wearable Companion

An interactive watch that allows users to read rules, follow scores for live games, and cheer for their favorite country. This idea was strong and targeted the problem effectively.

Concept B: The Gamified App

Gamification of the live-viewing experience with an enhanced cheer function, allowing users to interact with the main screen via their mobile phones.

Concept C: The Interactive Couch

A rounded couch focusing on group socialization with a cheer button and a small interface embedded into the furniture itself. This was deemed less feasible and resource-intensive.

Based on our concept evaluation and testing, we ended up combining the strongest ideas from all three initial storyboards‚Äîprimarily from Concept A‚Äîto create our final, refined solution.

Refinement

Concept Evaluation & Testing

We developed three distinct concepts based on our opportunity areas and used a decision matrix and user feedback to select the most viable and impactful solution. The interactive wearable concept was the clear winner.

Decision Matrix

We used a decision matrix to quantitatively score our concepts. Criteria were based on the problem statement, practicality, and usability, helping us identify the most logical path forward.

Concept Testing

We conducted early-stage concept testing to gather user feedback *before* prototyping. Asking questions about appeal and engagement validated that the wearable concept best enhanced the public viewing experience.
